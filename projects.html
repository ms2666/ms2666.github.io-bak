Sailing Simulator for Autonomous Sailboat

Prof. Andy Ruina, Mechanical Engineering
Cornell University

The goal of the Cornell Autonomous Sailboat Team is to decrease environmental research and acoustic marine mammal monitoring costs by developing a small, cheap, and mass-producible ocean sailboat. The boat will monitor ocean characteristics like turbidity, fluorescence, salinity, or acoustic patterns and navigate the open ocean for several years per service life. We are designing and manufacturing a one-meter long, durable, and fully autonomous sailing vessel that can intelligently navigate most regions of the ocean, collecting desired samples and relaying information back to an on-land computer all while powered exclusively with solar energy.
